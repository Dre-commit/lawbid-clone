Instruct a solicitor to assist with Personal & Commercial Debt Recovery

LawBid works with a nationwide panel of Debt Recovery Solicitors. Whether you require the assistance of a solicitor to recover a personal debt or a commercial debt for your business, our community of Debt Recovery Lawyers can help to get you the payments you require.

Unpaid invoices can have a strikingly detrimental impact on your business and your personal finances. Most of us would expect to receive payments on schedule and in the full amount owed. When debtors fail to make these payments the services of Debt Recovery Solicitors can be used to limit any financial damage to you or your business.

LawBid is the quick, simple and cost effective way to find Debt Recovery Solicitors to assist with your matter. Our innovative online portal puts the choice in your hands and allows you to choose the debt specialist who best suits your circumstances.

How Debt Solicitors Can Help

LawBid works with a nationwide community of Debt Recovery solicitors. With LawBid solicitors in Birmingham, Manchester, London, Newcastle, Cardiff, Glasgow, Edinburgh, Sheffield, Cambridge and many more locations across the UK, rest assured there is a solicitor for you.

The Debt Recovery Solicitors who work with LawBid can assist with both Commercial Debt Recovery and Personal Debt Recovery.

  • Commercial Debt Recovery
  • Personal Debt Recovery

The nature of your circumstances and the debtors willingness to cooperate will largely dictate how a Commercial Debt Recovery Solicitor approaches your matter. Below we have listed some of the services that a solicitor will be able to provide to businesses that require the recovery of unpaid debts:

  • An initial assessment of your situation
  • A letter before action
  • Issuing proceedings
  • Entering Judgments

Having a solicitor to offer you assistance, support and advice in the recovery of a personal debt can make be greatly beneficial. See the services that a Personal Debt Recovery Solicitor can provide below:

  • An initial assessment of your situation
  • Direct contact with the debtor
  • Debtor credit checking
  • Litigation and enforcement
